About

A short biography, told plainly.

I'm Wu Wenhan — a software engineer based in Shanghai with a soft spot for clean systems, considered writing, and long espresso conversations.

Where I come from

I studied software engineering at ECNU's Software Engineering Institute, where I learned to love the boring parts of the craft: writing tests, naming things, and building things small enough to throw away. My research interests drift around intelligent systems, learning analytics, and the messy middle of human–computer interaction.

What I work on

I build backend services and data tooling for the day job, and side-projects for the soul. I am most useful in the seam between research prototypes and production systems — the place where a paper becomes an API that does not crash on a Sunday.

Off the clock

I read more non-fiction than is probably healthy, brew pour-over coffee with embarrassing seriousness, and write short prose when the city goes quiet. I have been slowly learning to draw, mostly badly.

How to reach me

Email is best for anything that wants thought; you can find the address in the card on the right. For quick public threads, GitHub or LinkedIn work well. I generally reply within a few days.
